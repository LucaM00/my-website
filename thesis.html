The Cornerstone

Decoding the CHI3L1-RAGE Immunosuppressive Complex: Integrative Structural and Coevolutionary Insights into HER2+ Breast Cancer Resistance

The CHI3L1-RAGE complex represents a key mechanism in immunosuppression associated with HER2+ breast cancer. Through an integrated approach combining computational structural analysis, coevolutionary studies, and experimental validation, this thesis reveals new insights into the molecular mechanisms of therapeutic resistance. Structural analysis of the complex identified critical binding sites and allosteric conformations that modulate the activation of immunosuppressive pathways. Coevolutionary studies revealed conservation patterns suggesting the functional importance of specific protein regions. These results provide a solid foundation for the development of innovative therapeutic strategies aimed at overcoming resistance in HER2+ breast cancer.

The Story Behind the Science

This project represents the culmination of my integrated MEDTEC training, where rigorous engineering methodology meets the profound biological understanding of medicine. The thesis stems from the need to understand the molecular mechanisms that allow cancer to evade the immune system, a problem that requires a multidisciplinary approach to be effectively addressed. Through the integration of advanced computational techniques and experimental validation, I was able to build a comprehensive framework for understanding this complex biological system.
