Research

My research philosophy is based on the conviction that solving the most difficult problems requires an integrated, multi-pronged approach that connects the experimental and the computational. Each project represents an opportunity to build bridges between different disciplines.

Barcoding Neural Circuits with Base Editors

The Core Question: How can we trace and manipulate specific neural circuits with molecular precision?

The Integrated Approach: We combined high-throughput CRISPR screening in HEK293 cells with deep sequencing data analysis to develop a barcoding system that allows tracing of single neural cells across generations. The approach integrates gene editing techniques with advanced computational analysis.

The Key Insight: Molecular precision in gene editing can be leveraged to create biological "barcodes" that reveal the history and function of neural circuits, opening new possibilities for neurobiology and regenerative medicine.

CHI3L1-RAGE Complex in Cancer Resistance

The Core Question: How do immunosuppressive protein complexes contribute to therapeutic resistance in HER2+ breast cancer?

The Integrated Approach: We used an approach combining computational structural analysis, coevolutionary studies, and experimental validation to characterize the CHI3L1-RAGE complex. The integration of structural bioinformatics and molecular biology allowed the identification of new therapeutic targets.

The Key Insight: Structural understanding of immunosuppressive complexes can reveal previously hidden therapeutic vulnerabilities, offering new strategies to overcome drug resistance in cancer.

Computational Modeling of Biological Systems

The Core Question: How can we predict the behavior of complex biological systems through computational models?

The Integrated Approach: We develop mathematical models that integrate multi-omic experimental data with machine learning algorithms to predict cellular dynamics and therapeutic responses. The approach combines systems theory, big data analysis, and experimental validation.

The Key Insight: Computational models can serve as "telescopes" for biology, allowing us to see patterns and dynamics that would be invisible through direct experimental observation.
