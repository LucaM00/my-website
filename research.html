Research

My research philosophy is to approach complex problems with an integrated, multi-pronged framework that bridges the experimental and the computational. Each project is an effort to build a more complete model of a biological system.

Barcoding Neural Circuits with Base Editors

How can we create a high-diversity genetic recording system to map the architecture of the brain?

By combining high-throughput CRISPR screening in HEK293 cells with deep sequencing, we engineered a method to generate millions of unique genetic "barcodes." This work serves as a proof-of-concept for tracing the lineage and connectivity of single cells in complex biological systems like the brain.

The precision of gene editing can be scaled to create immense biological diversity, transforming it from a corrective tool into a powerful system for observation and mapping.

Decoding a Cancer Immunosuppressive Complex

How does the CHI3L1-RAGE protein complex contribute to therapeutic resistance in breast cancer?

This project integrated computational structural biology (AlphaFold2, PyMOL), co-evolutionary analysis, and experimental biophysics (mass photometry, crystallography) to build a high-confidence model of a previously intractable immunosuppressive interface, revealing its mechanism of action.

When direct observation is limited, a synthesis of computational modeling and experimental data can illuminate the structural logic of biology and reveal new therapeutic vulnerabilities.

Engineering Bacterial Metabolism

Can we rationally engineer the fundamental metabolic pathways of an organism for applied purposes?

Using the Lambda Red recombinase system, we engineered the genome of Salmonella enterica to create specific auxotrophic strains. This foundational work involved mastering core molecular biology techniques from plasmid purification to electroporation and PCR optimization.

Understanding and mastering the fundamental grammar of gene editing is the essential first step toward designing more complex and ambitious biological systems.
