The Cornerstone

Decoding the CHI3L1-RAGE Immunosuppressive Complex: Integrative Structural and Coevolutionary Insights into HER2+ Breast Cancer Resistance

The Story Behind the Science

This thesis is the culmination of my MEDTEC training, embodying the synthesis of engineering principles with deep biological inquiry. It began with a fundamental question: how do cancer cells build defenses against our therapies at a molecular level? By integrating advanced computational modeling with experimental validation, this work constructed a framework to understand a key piece of that puzzle, transforming an elusive mechanism into a structurally tractable target.

The interaction between chitinase-3-like protein 1 (CHI3L1) and its receptor RAGE is an emerging immune checkpoint in cancer. This work sought to define its architecture and logic as a basis for therapeutic intervention. Failing to resolve the complex crystallographically, we implemented a deep exploration of AlphaFold-Multimer predictions, yielding a high-confidence model of the interface. The model reveals that the positively charged FG-loop of RAGE inserts into CHI3L1’s conserved glycan-binding groove, locking the receptor in a non-signaling state. This work transforms a diffuse resistance mechanism into a structurally defined target and provides a template for rational inhibitor design.
