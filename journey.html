The Journey

The Origin

My foundation in classical studies was not a detour from science, but an education in seeing its architecture. It taught me to find the underlying structures in complex systems—a skill I now apply not to ancient texts, but to the code of life itself.

The Path of Exploration

The MEDTEC program was the turning point, the formal synthesis of medicine and engineering that gave my path its direction. From there, each research experience became a deliberate step in forging a multidisciplinary toolkit: mastering the grammar of gene editing at CSUN, using CRISPR to map the complexity of the brain at Harvard, and integrating computational models with wet-lab science at Stanford. Each step was chosen to build a more complete framework for solving biological problems.

The Grounding Force

My work in public health in Madagascar provided the essential "why." It grounded my research in tangible human needs, reinforcing the belief that the purpose of advancing science is to generate a concrete, meaningful impact on people's lives, especially in places where it is needed most.

The Vision Forward

These integrated experiences have solidified my mission. My path is now firmly oriented toward the horizon—to building new frameworks for understanding biology and engineering innovative solutions for the benefit of society and the clinic.
