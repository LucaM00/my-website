The Journey

The Origin

My path began not in a lab, but in the study of ancient languages and philosophy at Liceo Berchet, in Milan (Italy). There, I learned to see the world through the lens of structure—to find the hidden architecture in texts, arguments, and human history. That foundation taught me that to build anything new, you must first understand its deepest patterns.

The Collision of Worlds

The MEDTEC program was the crucible where my two worlds collided. It was here that the rigorous, systemic logic of engineering met the profound, organic complexity of medicine. I realized that the future of human health would not be found in one field, but in the disciplined, creative synthesis of both.

The Grounding Force

My work in public health in Madagascar provided the essential "why." It grounded my research in tangible human needs, reinforcing the belief that the purpose of advancing science is to generate a concrete, meaningful impact on people's lives, especially in places where it is needed most.

The Vision Forward

These integrated experiences have solidified my mission. My path is now firmly oriented toward the horizon—to building new frameworks for understanding biology and engineering innovative solutions for the benefit of society and the clinic.
